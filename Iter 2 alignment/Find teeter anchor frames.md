Find the anchor frame corresponding to the dog stepping off the teeter obstacle. A teeter obstacle rep consists of the dog jumping on the teeter and walking across it from right to left to climb it. Once the dog crosses the tipping point, the teeter tilts from pointing upwards to horizontal to pointing downwards and hitting the ground, at which point the dog steps or jumps off the obstacle. We need to find the frame where the dog steps off the teeter; specifically where the dog's hind legs leave the teeter. 
See the below progression on example frames:
Arya:
Arya-1 - Dog reached highest point on teeter, just before teeter tilt
Arya-2-Dog on horizontal teeter
Arya-3-Teeter finished tilting, dog facing downwards
Arya-4-Dog about to jump off teeter
Arya-5-Dog stepped off teeter
Arya-6-Hind legs leave teeter

Delta:
Delta-1-Dog reached highest point on teeter, just before teeter tilt
Delta-2-Dog on horizontal teeter
Delta-3-Teeter finished tilting, dog facing downwards
Delta-4-Dog about to jump off teeter
Delta-5-Dog stepped off teeter
Delta-6-Hind legs leave teeter

Izzy-1-Dog reached highest point on teeter, just before teeter tilt
Izzy-2-Dog on horizontal teeter
Izzy-3-Teeter finished tilting, dog facing downwards
Izzy-4-Dog about to jump off teeter
Izzy-5-Dog stepped off teeter
Izzy-6-Hind legs leave teeter

In the above examples we are primarily interested in finding 6- the frame where hind legs leave the teeter, but it is also useful to mark 5-where the dog stepped off the teeter, and 4-Dog about to jump off. It is also useful to roughly identify the region from 1-3 (dog reaches highest point just before teeter tilt - to teeter finished tilting).

Note that frames 1-5 must happen in that order.
